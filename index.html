Yuxuan Li 李宇轩

I'm a second-year PhD student in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute (HCII) within Carnegie Mellon University (CMU)'s School of Computer Science, advised by Hirokazu Shirado and Sauvik Das.

Humans increasingly delegate agency to AI agents in social contexts. My research studies social intelligence in AI agents and asks: how do we prepare for an agent-native future—and make that future useful today?

I approach this through two lenses:

Agents as social actors: evaluate and improve social intelligence in social interactions
EMNLP'25 Oral FAccT'25 ICML'26 Submission
Agents as social systems: use agent populations to simulate and inform real-world decisions
TOCHI Submission UIST'26 Submission USENIX Security'26 Submission PoliSim@CHI'26

I hold a BS in Computer Science from Tsinghua University, advised by Chun Yu and Yuanchun Shi. I was also a research intern at UC Berkeley, advised by Coye Cheshire. My research has received extensive media coverage and led to invited talks at institutions such as CMU, Georgia Tech, Tsinghua, HKU and Microsoft.
